<p>
ICITS is a conference about all aspects of information-theoretic
security and brings together the leading researchers in the areas of
cryptography, information theory and quantum information. It is the successor conference to the 2005 IEEE Information Theory Workshop on Theory and Practice in Information - Theoretic Security (ITW 2005) on Awaji Island, Japan. Previous ICITS conferences were held in:
</p>
<ul class="single">
<li><a href="http://www.spms.ntu.edu.sg/mas/conference/icits2013/">ICITS 2013</a> in Singapore.</li>
<li>ICITS 2012 in Montreal, Canada.</li>
<li><a href="http://event.cwi.nl/icits2011/">ICITS 2011</a> in Amsterdam, The Netherlands.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.rcis.aist.go.jp/ICITS2009/">ICITS 2009</a> in Shizuoka, Japan,</li>
<li><a href="http://www.iqis.org/events/icits2008/">ICITS 2008</a> in Calgary, Canada,</li>
<li><a href="http://www.escet.urjc.es/~matemati/maribel/ICITS/ITS07.htm">ICITS 2007</a> in Madrid, Spain,</li>
</ul>

<p>
ICITS 2015 will be held from <b>May 2 to 5</b>, and it takes place at <b><a href="http://www.usi.ch/">Universit&agrave; della Svizzera italiana (USI)</a></b> in Lugano, Switzerland.
</p>

<p>Topics of interest include (but are not restricted to):

<ul class="double">
<li>Physical layer security</li>
<li>Multiparty computations</li>
<li>Codes, lattices & cryptography</li>
<li>Authentication codes</li>
<li>Randomness extraction</li>
<li>Cryptography from noisy channels</li>
<li>Wiretap channels</li>
<li>Bounded-storage models</li>
<li>Information-theoretic reductions</li>
<li>Quantum cryptography</li>
<li>Quantum information theory</li>
<li>Nonlocality and nonsignaling</li>
<li>Key and message rates</li>
<li>Secret sharing</li>
<li>Physical models & assumptions</li>
<li>Network coding security</li>
<li>Adversarial channel models</li>
<li>Information-theoretic tools in computational settings</li>
<li>Implementation challenges</li>
<li>Biometric security</li>
</ul>

<p>
The proceedings of the conference will be published by Springer in Lecture Notes in Computer Science.
</p>
